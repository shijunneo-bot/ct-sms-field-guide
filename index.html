The SMS Field Guide

Colour tells you the vertical, the badge tells you the use case, and search finds any word in the copy. Click a card for the message and the full brief.

Start hereHow to use this gallery▾

This is a pattern library, not a template dump. Every card is a job SMS is genuinely good at, paired with the message, the trigger that makes it land, and whether it will deliver in your market. Here is how to turn a card into a live send.

1Find the fit

Filter by your vertical (the colour), or switch Group by to Vertical to browse a whole industry. Know the job already? Search a word: try “renewal”, “OTP”, or “abandoned”.

2Check the trigger

Each card leads with a timing signal: the event that makes the message relevant right now. If that signal is not live for your audience, it is not the right send yet.

3Confirm it delivers

Read the “Delivers in market” flags. Green: send as written. Amber: needs a registered Sender ID or a local rule. Red: the copy needs a market variant, usually link-free. Open the card for the compliant version.

4Adapt the copy

Hit the copy button on any message to grab the exact text, then swap [Brand] for your sender name, localise, and keep to one segment. An emoji flips SMS to Unicode at 70 characters per segment, so it costs more.

Badge = the use case Colour = the vertical delivers as written needs a rule / registration needs a market variant Pattern = one-off / recurring / automated / journey

SMS earns its place on reachability, speed, and no-app reach. Where WhatsApp or push already reaches your user well, lead with those and keep SMS for the moments only it wins. Each card’s “Why SMS, not WhatsApp” line makes that call for you.

ImplementBuild it in CleverTap▾

Found a pattern you like? Here is how it maps to the CleverTap SMS campaign builder, and what you can personalise once the copy is in.

The five steps
1Start here

Pick how users qualify: past behaviour or a custom list, live behaviour in real time, or an external API trigger. Confirm your SMS provider is connected, and optionally switch on conversion tracking.

2Who

Choose the target segment, or All Users, add rules, set a control group, and calculate estimated reach.

3What

Write the message. Paste a card’s copy here, then swap the placeholder for a personalisation token. The editor counts characters and SMS segments live.

4When

Send now, schedule for later, or set recurring, in your chosen timezone. Triggered and journey patterns take their timing from the trigger or the Journey.

5Publish

When every step shows green, publish the campaign.

The one thing that blocks everyone: the “What” step stays locked until an SMS service provider is connected to the account (Settings, then SMS). Until then you will see “No SMS Service Provider set.” That connection is where your Sender ID and the market’s delivery rules live, which is exactly what the compliance flag on each card is about.

Personalise the copy

The editor offers two token styles, from the {()} and @ buttons. They do the same job:

Name{{ Profile.name | default: "there" }}or  @Profile - Name
Phone{{ Profile.Phone | default: "" }}or  @Profile - Phone

So a line like “Hi [name], your order shipped” becomes “Hi {{ Profile.name | default: "there" }}, your order shipped.” Always set a default so the message still reads if the value is missing. Beyond Profile you can pull Event properties, live API values (Linked Content), Recommendations, and Catalog items. There is also an AI assist, the sparkle, to help draft or tighten a line.

CompareSMS vs WhatsApp vs RCS▾

RCS is the rich upgrade of SMS in the same inbox: cards, carousels, buttons and a verified brand, and it drops back to SMS where RCS is not live. That is why it sits here as a tab beside SMS, not as a separate gallery.

ChannelReachesRichnessThe catchBest for
SMSAny phone, no app, no internetText plus a link160 chars, no brandingReachability floor, OTP, time-critical, no-app reach

Pick SMS when you must reach anyone with minimal content. Pick WhatsApp for rich two-way in a high opt-in, WhatsApp-heavy market. Pick RCS for branded, richer messaging in the native inbox with SMS fallback, where your market and carriers support it.

SMS-plus: same job as a text, now with tappable buttons, formatting and a verified brand name.

Rich Card

media + title (200) + description (2000) + 4 buttons

One hero card. Vertical 3:1 or 2:1, or horizontal 3:4 (image left or right). Media static, dynamic per-user, or uploaded at campaign time.

Carousel

up to 10 swipeable cards

For product line-ups, recommendations and multi-offer. Vertical 4:5 / 5:4 or horizontal 2:1 / 4:3.

Buttons (max 4, rendering varies by device): URL Redirect (static, dynamic, or CleverTap click-tracking), Dialer (tap to call), Quick Reply (predefined tappable reply). Each logs a postback for analytics.

Sends like any CleverTap campaign: targeting, scheduling, personalisation ({{1}}, {{2}}) and SMS fallback. Stats cover delivery, click-through and engagement. RCS routes via Infobip; India needs pre-approved templates registered with the carrier (status within ~24h).

Common misreads: RCS is not WhatsApp (it is carrier-native, no separate app or opt-in app). RCS Business Messaging is not end-to-end encrypted, so keep OTPs and high-sensitivity content on an encrypted channel. And it is not universal, so always keep the SMS fallback on.

DecideIs this message transactional or promotional?▾

Classification follows the content and the trigger, not the channel or the intent behind the campaign. Work down the questions in order.

The trap sits at question 3. A delivery update with a discount code stapled to the end is a promotional message in most of these markets. Mixing the two does not make a promotional message transactional. It makes the whole message promotional, and it sends it on a route that was not registered for it.

ComplyHow APAC draws the line▾

Transactional messages often sit outside consent and quiet-hour restrictions, but only where they genuinely qualify. The registration regime, where one exists, is named below.

MarketFrameworkHow the line is drawn
IndiaTRAI, DLT registration registryEntities, sender IDs and templates are registered on the DLT portal. Both promotional and transactional senders must register. Promotional traffic is scrubbed against preference registers. URLs in marketing SMS must be pre-approved and whitelisted on DLT.
SingaporePDPA, DNC Registry, Spam Control Act registryMarketing messages need a DNC check no more than 30 days before sending. Transactional messages such as order confirmations, appointment reminders and account notifications carry no marketing content and are generally exempt. Sender IDs must be registered with the SMS Sender ID Registry, and unregistered IDs are relabelled as likely scam.
IndonesiaPersonal Data Protection Law (UU PDP No. 27 of 2022)Consent-based for marketing. Service messages tied to an existing agreement are treated separately. Sender ID registration is handled through the local operator or aggregator.
MalaysiaPDPA 2010, MCMC oversightConsent-based for direct marketing with an opt-out on request. Service and account messages sit outside the marketing definition.
ThailandPDPA B.E. 2562Consent-based for marketing, with a lawful basis such as contract performance available for service messages.
VietnamDecree 91/2020 registryPrior express consent for advertising messages, with a national DoNotCall list, per-day volume limits, a permitted sending window and a required advertising label. Sender IDs, content and URLs need registration. A pre-registered transaction route is offered for notification and OTP traffic.
PhilippinesData Privacy Act 2012, SIM Registration Act (RA 11934)Consent-based for marketing. SIM registration is mandatory, and commercial messaging rules on embedded links have tightened, so check current NTC guidance before sending links.
South KoreaPIPA and the Network Act labellingMarketing messages carry an advertising label in the header and unsubscribe details in the footer. Consent must be reconfirmed with recipients every two years. Alphanumeric sender IDs are not supported and are replaced with a long number.
JapanAct on Regulation of Transmission of Specified Electronic Mail, APPIOpt-in for advertising, with sender identification and an opt-out path. Service notices tied to a transaction are treated separately.
TaiwanPersonal Data Protection ActMarketing must offer a free opt-out, and it must be honoured on first request. Service messages fall outside that duty.
Australia and New ZealandSpam Act 2003, Unsolicited Electronic Messages Act 2007Consent, sender identification and a working unsubscribe are required for commercial messages. Purely factual service messages have a narrower obligation, though sender identification still applies.

Working rule for APAC: the safest test is content, not intent. If a compliance officer would read one line of the message as marketing, treat the whole message as promotional and route it accordingly. This page is a working reference rather than legal advice, so confirm current rules with your counsel and your provider before a launch.

LimitsWhat it is not, and where it gets abused▾

It is not a way around consent

A transactional route carries service content. It does not turn a marketing message into a permitted one, and it does not extend your consent basis.

It is not a quiet-hours bypass

Some markets allow service messages outside normal windows. That allowance follows the content and the local rule, not the route you picked in a dashboard.

It is not a cheaper promotional channel

Sending offers on a transactional route to reduce cost or improve delivery is misclassification. It puts the sender ID, the template registration and the account at risk.

It is not a reason to drop opt-out

Where a service message sits outside marketing opt-out, that is a narrow exemption. A message that mixes in an offer loses it.

One offer contaminates the message

A single promotional line in an otherwise factual alert is usually enough to reclassify the whole message in these markets.

Registration is per template

Editing approved copy can invalidate the registration. Content changes go back through the registry before they go out.

Q&ACommon questions▾

Can we send an OTP and a promotion in the same message?

Treat that as promotional. The offer sets the classification, so it needs the promotional route, the consent basis and the registry check that go with it.

Does transactional mean we can ignore Do Not Disturb?

Not by default. DND is a campaign setting in CleverTap, and switching it off is a compliance decision that depends on the market and on the message genuinely being a service message.

Do we still need DLT registration for transactional SMS in India?

Yes. Both promotional and transactional senders register on the DLT portal, with the entity, sender ID and templates recorded there.

Is a delivery update exempt from Singapore's DNC Registry?

A delivery update with no marketing content is generally treated as transactional and sits outside the DNC obligation. Add an offer and the exemption no longer applies.

What can we tell a customer about Notify SMS today?

That transactional sending is supported through SMS Type configuration today, and that Notify SMS specifics are pending confirmation. Nothing about tier, pricing or a separate route should be promised until the product team confirms it.

Why is there no use case gallery here?

Transactional SMS has little visual variety and a great deal of mechanical and regulatory difference. The comparison, the decision flow and the market rules carry more value than a set of near-identical cards.
